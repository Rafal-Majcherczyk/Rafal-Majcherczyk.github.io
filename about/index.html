ABOUT

Hello! My name is Rafal Majcherczyk. My passion for numbers and quantification led me to study mathematics at AGH University of Science and Technology, where I earned a Master of Science in Applied Mathematics with a major in Financial and Insurance Mathematics. After graduation, I spent several years working in the financial sector in various analytical and managerial positions. During this time, I had an opportunity to work on numerous projects, including initiatives with high-level executive visibility. I was fortunate to be part of an international group that serviced clients globally, which later led me to manage several talented teams. This allowed me to collaborate with people from all around the world, primarily from North America, Europe, Asia, and Oceania.

After many fulfilling years in the industry, I felt the time for change had come. I decided to take a step back, which led me to a career break. While projects and initiatives that I was involved in were extremely satisfying, I felt a growing disconnect with my analytical nature as I climbed higher in my career. I realized how much joy I used to derive from learning statistics, probability theory, and programming (I even wrote my master’s thesis on image deblurring with a set of simulations written in R). I started to look for a new challenge.

In the meantime, the pandemic hit us all hard, changing our customs and situations radically and leaving billions of people in disbelief. Analyzing various medical reports, statements from different experts and observing social disputes, I once again noticed how overloaded we are with information yet unable to make decisions backed by facts. I think that E.O. Wilson’s quote, which Tony Robbins reminded me of during one of his seminars, sums it up perfectly: “We are drowning in information while starving for wisdom.” I had to acknowledge that, despite my analytical background, I have struggled as much as everyone else to get to the essence of the endless data available to us, data that is growing exponentially.

The belief that the vast amount of information readily available to us thanks to the digital revolution should be a blessing and not the cause of confusion and anxiety led me to the data industry. Researching the topic got me fascinated and convinced me to return to my analytical roots and spend the following years mastering various skills relevant to this field to help others and myself make informed decisions based on data-driven insights as we navigate a rapidly evolving reality. My journey will be documented on this website.
